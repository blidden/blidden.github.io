My Childs eHealth Record - AUS Digital Health Agency

Working in conjunction with the Australian Government and Accenture to build an app for new Australian parents.

History of Child Development Record Keeping in Australia

All children grow and develop at different rates. It is important for parents to monitor their child’s growth and development so that any possible concerns can be identified and treated as early as possible. For decades the ‘Blue Book’ has been the Australian standard, authorised and distributed by the Australian Government to all first-time or returning parents of newborns.

Record keeping in the Blue Book is a manual process, often done after milestone appointments with the child’s doctor.

My Child’s eHealth Record

During my time with Deloitte Digital I was resourced as a UX designer for the project, built in part by Accenture (back-end Government records) and the Deloitte Digital team. The app wasn’t designed replace the Blue Book or its equivalents, but moreso as a companion piece to the new-parent journey (i.e. if a parent was asked by a childcare centre about their child’s immunisation status, they’d be likely to have their mobile with them even if they are not carrying the blue book)

This was 2013, so the design standards for both iOS and Android have vastly changed. Reflecting on the project now, though, the team and I strove to ensure design standards were adhered to at the time. Amidst the earlier years of mobile app design and the often chaotic nature of aesthetic trends of the time, the design and UX balanced contemporary thinking with a user journey suited for new users to smartphones (still growing in number in that historical moment).

The app connected parents to their child’s eHealth record, which, at the time, was a new function added recently as part of the ongoing rollout of the Government’s national eHealth record system - now a standard in Australia. This presented a challenge in that the design was theoretically a data-first approach. The team had to ensure all data points provided by the Government portal were accessible via the app.

Some of the data points included personal measurements for a baby or young child’s head circumference, height and weight, information and reminders about immunisations and child health checks, and observations by parents about their children’s personal growth and achievements. These updates would provide the parents with useful info when consulting with the child’s doctor. The data was both exposed for viewing as well as manually entering (or editing) to see how the baby was growing over time.
